Howard Zinn

Here's a time-line of Professor Howard Zinn's life:

    1922 - On August 24th, Howard Zinn is born. He grows up in New York City.

    1943 - Zinn joins the Army Air Corps in 1943, eager to fight the fascists, and becomes a bombardier in a B-17.

    1944 - Zinn marries Roslyn Shechter.

    1947 - Their daughter Myla is born.

    1949 - Their son Jeff is born and they move to public housing while Zinn attends college on the G.I. bill. While doing back breaking work as a warehouse loader and with Roz working part-time while raising two small> children, Zinn earns a B.A. at NYU and Master's and Doctoral degrees at Columbia University.

    1956 - Zinn is offered a job at Spelman College, a historically black women’s college, as chair of the history department. Zinn, along with Ella Baker, becomes an "adult adviser" to the Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee(SNCC) marching for civil rights with his students, angering Spelman's president.

    1959 - Zinn's first book "La Guardia in Congress" receives an honorable mention in the competition for the American Historical Association's Albert J. Beveridge Award.

    1963 - Spelman's president notifies Zinn that his "services would no longer be needed" even though he was still under tenured contract. In October, Zinn traveles to Selma, Alabama as an observer of the voter registration campaign, documenting the day in great detail.

    1964 - Zinn starts teaching political science at Boston University.

    1965 - Writing for The Nation, Zinn travels to Selma for the final stretch of the march from Selma to Montgomery in March.

    1968 - Zinn travels with the Rev. Daniel Berrigan to Hanoi to receive prisoners released by theNorth Vietnamese and writes "Disobedience and Democracy: Nine Fallacies of Law and Order".

    1979 - Boston University faculty, secretaries, staff, and librarians go on strike at various times. Zinn is one of the co-chairs of the strike committee of the faculty union.

    1988 - Zinn retires from teaching concluding his last class early so he could join a picket line at BU's School of Nursing. He invited his students to join him.

    2010 - Howard Zinn dies on January 27, 2010 in Santa Monica, California from a heart attack.
